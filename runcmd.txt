1. The Immediate Fix (The Bridge)
Keep your Metro terminal running. Open a new, second PowerShell window and run this exact command:

PowerShell
adb reverse tcp:8081 tcp:8081
If that returns an error like device not found, your USB cable is loose or "USB Debugging" turned itself off on your phone. Unplug the cable, plug it back in, and make sure you see the "Allow USB Debugging?" prompt on your phone screen.

2. The "Nuclear" Restart
If adb reverse doesn't fix it instantly, the ADB server itself has crashed. Run this sequence in your terminal:

PowerShell

adb kill-server
adb start-server

adb reverse tcp:8081 tcp:8081
3. Verification
Once you run those, go back to your phone and Shake the device to ope

adb reverse tcp:8081 tcp:8081


If you see the QR code but the app won't open or you get the "Refused" error, run these three commands in order:

1. Kill the Ghosts
This clears out any dead connections to emu-5562.

Bash

adb kill-server

adb start-server

2. Confirm your Phone is "Seen"
Make sure your Samsung ID shows up here.

Bash
adb devices

It should say RZCY20NBCJJ  device. If it says offline, unplug the USB and plug it back in.

3. Bridge the Gap (The "Magic" Command)


This tells your phone that port 8081 (the Metro Bundler) is on your computer.

Bash
adb -s RZCY20NBCJJ reverse tcp:8081 tcp:8081

DO NOT press a (Open on Android): This often triggers the search for an emulator and causes the 5562 error you just saw.

DO NOT use the camera to scan: Since you are using a custom Development Build (not Expo Go), scanning the QR with your camera won't always work correctly.

THE SLICK MOVE: Simply tap the NomadMeet icon already installed on your Samsung.

Because you ran the adb reverse command in Step 3, the app will automatically connect to your computer and start loading the JavaScript.

If it doesn't load immediately, Shake the Phone and select "Reload."
